I am **Ahmad Hasan Sakib**, a Physics Researcher at **Shahjalal University of Science and Technology (SUST)**. My work is dedicated to exploring the fundamental architecture of the universe through the lens of **Computational Physics** and **Artificial Intelligence**.

### Research Philosophy
I believe that the next frontier of physical discovery lies at the intersection of classical theory and data-driven intelligence. By leveraging **Machine Learning**, we can decode complex nonlinear phenomena that were previously computationally inaccessible, bridging the gap between abstract mathematical models and empirical reality.

### Education
*   **B.Sc. in Physics**, Shahjalal University of Science and Technology (SUST)
    *   Focus: Nonlinear Optics, Computational Physics, and Mathematical Modeling.

### Core Research Interests
*   **Nonlinear Optics (NLO):** Light-matter interaction, soliton dynamics, and ultrafast optics.
*   **Machine Learning in Physics:** Neural network architectures for physical system modeling (PINNs).
*   **Computational Methods:** High-performance simulations using C++, Python, and Julia.

### Technical Expertise
*   **Programming:** Python (PyTorch, NumPy), C/C++, Julia, LaTeX.
*   **Tools:** Git, Mathematica, ROOT (CERN), Linux environments.
*   **Methodologies:** Numerical integration (RK4), Monte Carlo simulations, Deep Learning.

### Academic Profiles & Contact
I am open to collaborative research projects and scientific discussions.
